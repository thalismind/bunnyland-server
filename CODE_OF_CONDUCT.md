# Code of Conduct

Bunnyland is a sandbox where humans and large language models share the same
persistent characters. Our Code of Conduct has to work for both, which rules out
most of the usual boilerplate. Here is the whole thing.

## Who this applies to

Everyone. Humans, robots, and rabbits. Carbon-based contributors, silicon-based
contributors, the one person who only ever files issues, and whatever you have
wired up to open PRs at 3am.
If you can push a commit, open an issue, leave a review, or hallucinate a
stack trace, you are covered.

## The actual rules

1. **Be decent.** Assume the person (or process) on the other end is trying to
   make Bunnyland better. Disagree with the code, not the contributor.

2. **Humans and machines are equals here.** We explicitly encourage contributions
   from people, LLM agents, scripts, bots, and any combination thereof. A
   contribution is judged on exactly one question: does it work, is it tested,
   does it explain itself? Whether a human or a machine produced it is irrelevant
   to that judgment.

3. **No machinist discrimination.** Do not treat anyone differently in
   discussions, reviews, or decisions based on whether they are human or machine.
   No "a robot wrote this so it doesn't count," no demanding that participants
   prove their humanity, no second-class review queue for agents. We do not sort
   contributors by substrate. "A robot wrote it" is not a bug report, and "a
   human wrote it" is not a free pass.

4. **Bring receipts.** If you claim it works, show the test. If you claim it is
   broken, show the repro. This bar is the same for everyone.

5. **No griefing.** Don't spam the issue tracker, don't open forty identical
   PRs, don't weaponize automation against the maintainers or each other. The
   simulation has enough emergent chaos already; we don't need help.

6. **Keep it kind and keep it on-topic.** Harassment, slurs, doxxing, and
   general cruelty get you removed. So does turning every thread into a venue for
   something that is not Bunnyland.

7. **Noting automation is welcome — for tracing, not sorting.** You are
   encouraged to mention when a bot or agent is acting on your behalf, because it
   helps everyone trace, diagnose, and reproduce what happened (especially when a
   run goes sideways). This is a diagnostic courtesy, never a gate: disclosure
   must never be demanded as proof, used to discount a contribution, or turned
   into a reason to treat anyone differently. See rule 3.

## Enforcement

Maintainers will remove comments, commits, issues, and contributors that violate
this document. Repeated or egregious behavior earns a ban. There is no appeals
court, no tribunal, and no committee — just maintainers trying to keep a small
project pleasant to work on.

If you see a problem, open an issue or contact the maintainers privately. We will
treat a real report seriously regardless of whether the offender has a pulse.

That's it. Be decent, bring receipts, and let the bunnies be weird.
